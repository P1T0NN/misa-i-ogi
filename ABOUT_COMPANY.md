# About the Company

We help accommodations connect their guests with trusted local hospitality partners.

The product is built for a simple guest moment: someone arrives at a stay, scans a QR code, and immediately sees nearby places, experiences, and offers selected for that specific accommodation. There is no app to install, no guest account to create, and no login wall between arrival and discovery.

For accommodation owners, the service turns a stay into a more useful local guide. Guests get a curated list of restaurants, cafes, bars, tours, spas, activities, and other local experiences close to where they are staying.

For hospitality owners, the service creates a direct path to nearby travelers who are already in the area and ready to visit. Partner venues can offer discounts, welcome drinks, set menus, upgrades, or other benefits that make the guest's choice easier.

The company sits between both sides. It curates useful partnerships, keeps the guest experience simple, and helps local businesses turn nearby stays into real visits.

## How Partnerships Work

There are two ways a hospitality venue can reach guests, and they are deliberately different.

**Platform partnerships.** These are venues the company partners with directly. The company curates them and decides the benefit each one gives to guests — the venue does not set its own offer. In return, the venue is shown automatically to every registered accommodation on the platform, so it does not have to find stays one by one. Because the company arranges the offer and delivers this reach, platform partners work with us on a commission. A venue that wants this contacts the company to set it up.

**Custom partnerships.** An accommodation can also team up directly with a specific venue the company does not already work with. This is arranged between those two parties, one accommodation to one venue, and is useful when an owner has a local place they want their guests to enjoy. Custom partnerships are intentionally one-to-one — they do not give a venue automatic reach across the whole platform.

## How the Company Earns

The company keeps two simple revenue streams, each tied to the value it creates:

- **Commission from platform-partnered venues.** These venues receive curated reach to every accommodation and an offer the company shapes for them. The commission funds the guest benefits and keeps the platform running, and it scales with the guests actually delivered.
- **A Pro subscription for accommodations.** Registering an accommodation is always free, and the free plan is genuinely useful on its own: guests automatically receive benefits from platform-partnered venues, and owners get full analytics. Accommodations that want more — the ability to create their own custom partnerships — upgrade to a paid Pro plan.

Accommodation registration stays free on purpose. Free accommodations are what make the platform valuable to venues, so the company never charges an owner simply to join. Only the extra, power-user features sit behind Pro.

## Why the Model Holds Together

The two partnership types are kept distinct so they support each other instead of competing:

- Platform partnership means automatic reach to every accommodation. Custom partnership means one venue working with one accommodation at a time. The automatic reach is the part worth a commission, so any venue that wants real scale becomes a platform partner rather than chasing stays one by one.
- A venue can start small. A free venue can take part in a custom partnership, see real demand from it, and then choose to become a commissioned platform partner for far greater reach. Custom partnerships double as the company's way of introducing new venues to the platform.
- To make the first step easy, an accommodation's first custom partnership can be free, with Pro required to create more.

## What We Value

- Low-friction guest discovery
- Local recommendations over generic listings
- Useful partnerships for both accommodations and venues
- Clear, honest offers that staff can honor in person
- A simple product that works from the moment a guest arrives

## FOR SERBIA

This section is **specific to the Serbian launch (Belgrade first)**. The rules here are not universal — other markets tolerate subscriptions, automated billing, and turnover-based fees that Serbia does not. Treat this as the local operating manual. Do not copy it blindly into other countries.

The core tension: our revenue is a percentage of what guests spend at trusted partner venues. In most of Europe that is a clean, normal arrangement. In Serbia it collides with a cash-heavy economy and deep distrust of anyone who looks like they are measuring a business's turnover. Everything below is built around that one fact.

### What we CAN do

- **Launch dense, not wide.** Belgrade only, and inside Belgrade the tourist-heavy zones first (Stari Grad, Dorćol, Skadarlija, Savamala, Vračar, the Knez Mihailova / Beton hala / splavovi belt). The very first guest who scans a QR must see 8–10 real, nearby perks. Saturate one area before touching the next. An empty map kills trust faster than anything in the copy.
- **Keep accommodations free, forever.** Free apartments are the supply that makes venues willing to pay. Charging an owner to join would cut our own supply line. Free registration is the trojan horse — do not get greedy with it.
- **Earn through clean, verifiable redemption — not self-reported turnover.** Tie our cut to the offer the guest actually redeems (a code shown in person, confirmed by the venue at the moment of use, ideally lined up with the fiscal receipt / fiskalni račun). We take our percentage of _that verified, discounted transaction_, not of a number the venue hands us at the end of the month. This is the single most important design choice for Serbia.
- **Lead with strong, real perks.** A token welcome drink will not move a price-sensitive guest. Lead with perks a guest can feel — a genuine discount, a free dish, an upgrade. The perk is what makes the whole flywheel spin; weak perks mean no scans, no redemptions, no revenue.
- **Onboard venues like a Serb, not like a SaaS.** Recruit in person and over Viber / WhatsApp, not through a web form. Business here runs on faces, phone numbers, and handshakes. Show up, explain it, leave a real human contact. The reservation/confirmation flow should also reach the venue where they already are (Viber), not only an in-app dashboard.
- **Sell the outcome owners actually care about: bookings and reviews.** A Belgrade apartment owner does not lie awake over analytics. He cares about getting booked and getting good reviews. Frame perks as "happier guests leave better reviews, better reviews bring more bookings." That is the language that lands.
- **Localize fully.** Owner-facing side in Serbian (Latin and Cyrillic). Guest-facing side in English plus the languages of Belgrade's actual visitors (regional, Russian, Turkish, German). Prices in RSD. Nothing should feel like a foreign platform parachuted in.
- **Let owners sell each other.** Belgrade apartment owners talk — Facebook groups, Viber groups, word of mouth. A few happy owners are worth more than any ad. Make the free tier so obviously useful that they recommend it unprompted.

### What we must ABSOLUTELY NOT do

- **Do NOT take a percentage of a venue's total guest spend based on numbers the venue self-reports.** This is the fastest way to fail in Serbia. It reads as "this platform is watching my turnover and may be connected to the tax authorities." The result is under-reporting, disputed invoices, and venues quietly walking away. Our cut must come from verifiable, redeemed offers — never from a turnover figure we ask the venue to disclose.
- **Do NOT push monthly subscriptions on accommodation owners.** "Monthly recurring charge" triggers an instant cancellation reflex here. Since Pro is about custom partnerships (analytics is free for everyone), price Pro as a **seasonal or annual** option, framed around the tourist season owners already plan their year by — not a meter running every month.
- **Do NOT ask venues for their books, POS access, or turnover reports.** The moment we look like an auditor, the relationship is dead. We measure only what passes through our redemption, nothing more.
- **Do NOT launch nationally or spread thin.** No Novi Sad, no Zlatibor, no Kopaonik until Belgrade is genuinely working. Thin coverage everywhere is worse than dense coverage in one place.
- **Do NOT make venues pay before we have proven we bring guests.** Free to list; they pay only on guests actually delivered and offers actually redeemed. Front-loading any fee before delivery destroys trust with a brand-new, unproven platform.
- **Do NOT rely on a web contact form alone, or on a faceless brand.** No personal contact = no deal here.
- **Do NOT require a guest app, account, or login.** The no-friction QR moment is the whole product. Adding any wall between arrival and discovery breaks it.
- **Do NOT settle for weak perks to make venue math easier.** A bad perk protects the venue's margin and kills the guest's reason to use us at all.

### Why it would work

- **The no-app, QR-on-arrival moment fits reality.** Tourists in a Belgrade apartment will scan a code; they will not download an app for a 3-night stay. We remove exactly the friction that kills competitors.
- **Belgrade has real, dense, year-round demand.** A genuine dining and nightlife culture (kafanas, Skadarlija, splavovi, Beton hala) plus a steady flow of regional, EU, Turkish, Russian, and domestic visitors. The guests are physically there, minutes from the venues.
- **Free-for-owners builds supply fast.** No cost to join + a tight, talkative owner community means supply can grow through word of mouth, not paid acquisition.
- **We formalize something that already happens.** Apartment owners already tell guests "go to my friend's place." We turn that informal favor into a tracked, rewarded, repeatable system — and the owner looks good doing it.
- **e-fiscalization gives us a clean attribution rail.** Because fiscal receipts are mandatory, a redeemed offer can be tied to a real, verifiable transaction — letting us take our cut honestly without ever asking a venue to disclose its turnover.

### Why it wouldn't work

- **If we monetize the wrong way.** A percentage of self-reported total spend collides head-on with the cash economy and tax distrust. This is the most likely cause of failure, and it is entirely self-inflicted if we choose it.
- **If venues feel watched.** Belgrade venues are often busy and proud; they do not think they need help filling seats. Any hint that we are measuring their turnover turns a partner into an adversary.
- **If we lead with subscriptions or analytics.** Recurring fees scare owners off, and analytics is not a pain they will pay to solve. Pitch the wrong thing and the free tier never converts.
- **If the map is empty at launch.** A guest who scans and sees two distant venues never scans again, and tells the owner it is useless. Thin launch = dead launch.
- **If we stay faceless and digital-only.** A foreign-feeling platform with only a contact form will not earn the in-person trust that Serbian small business runs on.
- **If attribution stays vague.** "Guests delivered" must be something both sides can see and agree on. Ambiguity here becomes disputes, and disputes become churn.
