## Conversion Goal

The landing page should make serious accommodation and hospitality owners feel that this is a premium, practical guest-demand network worth contacting us about.

Primary conversion:

- Contact us through an in-page `#contact` section.

Secondary conversions:

- Register free through `/signup`.
- View the guest experience through `/demo`.

The page should not feel like a generic SaaS product page. It should feel like a polished hospitality sales page for owners who care about guest experience, reputation, and local revenue.

## Core Message

A guest benefits network for stays and hospitality.

For accommodation owners:

- Register an accommodation for free.
- Place one QR code inside the room, apartment, villa, or hotel.
- Give guests curated nearby benefits from selected hospitality partners.
- Make the stay feel more useful without building a concierge team.
- Track QR scans, guest activations, and reservation demand from the owner dashboard.

For hospitality owners:

- Receive guests who are already nearby and actively deciding where to go.
- Show a concrete perk, discount, welcome drink, menu, upgrade, or experience.
- Let guests request reservations from the hospitality page.
- Confirm, decline, and track requests from the dashboard.
- See which accommodations are sending demand.

Shared promise:

- No guest app.
- No guest account.
- No generic city guide.
- A curated flow from room QR to local benefit to reservation request.

## Page Strategy

The page should give equal narrative weight to accommodation owners and hospitality owners.

Recommended conversion order:

1. Show the guest moment first: a QR code in a premium room unlocks a useful local benefits page.
2. Explain the two-sided value: accommodation owners improve the stay, hospitality owners receive nearby demand.
3. Prove the flow visually with five concrete steps.
4. Prove the owner value with analytics, partnerships, and reservation management.
5. Close with a high-touch contact section and a free registration fallback.

Primary CTA language:

- `Contact us`
- `Talk to us`
- `Plan a partnership`

Secondary CTA language:

- `Register free`
- `View guest demo`

Avoid:

- Invented business numbers.
- "Enormous amount" as literal copy. Use stronger, cleaner phrasing like "new nearby demand" or "guests already minutes away."
- Generic "all-in-one platform" language.
- Big vague claims without showing the exact product moment.

## Visual Direction

Follow `DESIGN.html` as the source of truth.

Required design traits:

- Warm parchment background.
- Iowan Old Style / Charter / Georgia style serif headings at weight 500.
- System sans body text.
- Terracotta accent for eyebrows and primary CTA only.
- Editorial pacing with generous vertical rhythm.
- Alternating light and dark chapters.
- Real product proof through screenshots, phone mockups, dashboard mockups, QR visuals, and hospitality imagery.

Hero visual direction:

- Use a full-bleed premium room or apartment entry image.
- The image must clearly imply arrival: doorway, room, bedside table, entry console, welcome card, or QR card.
- Text sits directly over the image, not inside a floating card.
- The first viewport must show the product category immediately: stays, guests, QR benefits, hospitality network.
- Leave a hint of the next section visible on desktop and mobile.

Design constraints:

- Do not make a centered generic hero with a small illustration.
- Do not create a page made of repeated identical cards.
- Do not put cards inside cards.
- Do not use gradient text.
- Do not use cool blue-gray palettes.
- Do not use pure white as the page background.
- Do not overuse rounded SaaS panels.

## Landing Page Sections

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- Make owners understand the product in five seconds.
- Establish premium hospitality credibility.
- Give both audiences a reason to continue.

Working headline:

> Turn every stay into a direct path to the best places nearby.

Supporting copy:

> Guests scan one QR code in the room, unlock curated local benefits, and request reservations from partner hospitality venues. Accommodations join free. Hospitality partners receive guests who are already minutes away.

CTA stack:

- Primary button: `Contact us` linking to `#contact`.
- Secondary button: `Register free` linking to `/signup`.
- Text link: `View guest demo` linking to `/demo`.

Hero proof points:

- `No guest app`
- `Free for accommodations`
- `Curated hospitality partners`
- `Owner analytics`

Visual:

- Full-bleed premium accommodation photo with an overlaid QR/welcome card treatment.
- Optional small phone mockup partially visible on the side showing a guest stay page.
- Keep the phone/QR product detail visible enough to avoid a purely atmospheric hero.

### 2. Convincing Dual-Audience Section

Purpose:

- Explain the marketplace value without making visitors choose too early.
- Make accommodation owners and hospitality owners both feel directly addressed.

Section title:

> Two owners, one guest moment.

Structure:

- Left side: accommodation owner value.
- Center: the guest scan moment.
- Right side: hospitality owner value.

Accommodation copy:

> Your stay gets a simple, premium local benefits page. Guests scan the QR code, see selected nearby places, and feel that your accommodation gave them a better trip. Registration is free.

Accommodation bullets:

- Free accommodation registration.
- Property-specific QR code.
- Curated nearby hospitality benefits.
- Better guest experience without extra staff.
- Scan and reservation analytics.

Hospitality copy:

> Your venue appears when a nearby guest is actively deciding where to eat, drink, relax, or book an experience. The offer gives them a reason to choose you now.

Hospitality bullets:

- Guests already staying nearby.
- Perks, discounts, welcome drinks, menus, upgrades, or experiences.
- Reservation request flow.
- Confirm or decline each request.
- Source-stay and demand analytics.

Visual:

- Not two flat cards only.
- Use a horizontal "guest path" composition: `Room QR -> guest benefits page -> hospitality reservation request`.
- Use two owner columns around it, with the guest moment as the bridge.

### 3. Five-Step Guest Flow

Purpose:

- Make the product feel real and understandable.
- Turn the abstract marketplace into a concrete owner and guest story.

Section title:

> From room scan to reservation request.

Step 1: QR code waits in the accommodation

- Image: premium apartment or hotel room with a visible QR card.
- Copy: `A guest arrives and finds one QR card inside the stay. No app, no account, no reception explanation needed.`

Step 2: Guest opens the stay benefits page

- Image: screenshot or phone mockup of the guest stay page.
- Copy: `The page is tied to that accommodation and shows benefits selected for that exact stay. Guests see nearby hospitality partners and what each one offers.`

Step 3: Guest selects a hospitality partner

- Image: hospitality detail page with cover image, address, type, description, and benefit context.
- Copy: `The guest opens a partner page, checks the offer, location, and details, then decides if it fits their plan.`

Step 4: Guest requests a reservation

- Image: reservation form with guest name, guest count, preferred time, phone, and optional email.
- Copy: `If the hospitality accepts reservations, the guest sends a short request directly from the page.`

Step 5: Request is sent and tracked

- Image: pending reservation card or owner reservation dashboard.
- Copy: `The hospitality owner receives the request, contacts the guest if needed, and confirms or declines it. The guest sees the request status, and the owner keeps the request in the dashboard.`

Interaction idea:

- Desktop: cinematic horizontal timeline with one dominant active image and five short step markers.
- Mobile: vertical story with one image per step.

Important:

- This section should use actual product screenshots when implementation begins.
- If screenshots are not ready, build faithful mockups from existing components and label internal mock data clearly.

### 4. Features: Unified Owner Analytics

Purpose:

- Show that this is not only a guest-facing QR page.
- Prove that owners receive operational visibility.

Section title:

> See where guest demand is moving.

Core idea:

- Use one analytics dashboard visual with an audience switch: `Accommodation view` and `Hospitality view`.
- The chart data can be dummy example data, but must be labeled `Example dashboard data`.

Accommodation analytics view:

- Metrics:
  - QR scans.
  - Guest activations.
  - Reservation requests.
  - Top hospitality partners.
- Table concept:
  - Partner hospitality.
  - Views.
  - Reservation requests.
  - Confirmed requests.

Hospitality analytics view:

- Metrics:
  - Guest views.
  - Guest activations.
  - Reservation requests.
  - Source accommodations.
- Table concept:
  - Source accommodation.
  - Views.
  - Reservation requests.
  - Confirmed requests.
  - Conversion.

Visual:

- Use a dashboard screenshot/mock with real chart shapes.
- Use existing chart components in later implementation.
- Keep it sophisticated and quiet, not a rainbow analytics section.
- Use terracotta for the main chart line or bar, warm gray for comparison data.

Copy:

> Accommodations can see which stays create engagement. Hospitality owners can see which connected stays create demand. Everyone gets the same truth: what guests actually open, request, and use.

### 5. Features: Custom Partnerships

Purpose:

- Explain that partnerships are specific and curated, not a generic directory.
- Show why accommodation and hospitality owners want to be matched.

Section title:

> Partnerships are local, specific, and editable.

Copy:

> A stay can be connected to selected hospitality partners, and a hospitality partner can work with selected stays. The offer can be a percent discount or a non-percent benefit like a welcome drink, set menu, upgrade, or experience.

Feature points:

- Accommodation-to-hospitality matching.
- Hospitality-to-accommodation matching.
- Optional discount percentage.
- Non-percent perks supported through listing copy.
- Partnership list is curated per accommodation.
- Guests only see benefits connected to their stay.

Implementation note:

- Current admin UI supports creating partnerships with accommodation, one or more hospitalities, and optional discount percentage.
- If owner self-service partnership creation is not intended for launch, landing copy should say `We help set up the right partnerships` rather than promising full self-service matching.

Visual:

- Show a split owner panel:
  - Accommodation selected on the left.
  - Hospitality partners selected on the right.
  - Offer field in the center.
- Make the section feel like curation, not database administration.

### 6. Features: Reservation Management

Purpose:

- Show hospitality owners what happens after a guest sends a request.
- Reassure accommodation owners that the guest is not left in a dead-end flow.

Section title:

> Reservation requests do not disappear into a form.

Copy:

> When a guest sends a request, the hospitality owner can review it, contact the guest, confirm it, decline it, or mark the outcome later. The guest sees whether the request is pending or confirmed.

Feature points:

- Pending requests.
- Confirmed reservations.
- Cancelled or declined requests.
- No-show tracking.
- Guest phone and optional email.
- Contact via WhatsApp or phone where relevant.

Visual:

- Use a compact reservation workflow:
  - `Pending`
  - `Confirmed`
  - `Cancelled`
  - `No-show`
- Include one request row with guest name, time, guest count, source hospitality, and action buttons.

Tone:

- Operational and trustworthy.
- Avoid overpromising instant confirmation.
- Explain that the hospitality owner contacts the guest to confirm when needed.

### 7. Trust Standards

Purpose:

- Establish quality control for larger owners and famous venues.
- Make the network feel curated and premium.

Section title:

> Curated means both sides look good.

Standards:

- Walking-distance-first recommendations.
- Real benefits staff can honor in person.
- No guest app.
- No guest account.
- No ad-ranked directory.
- Property-specific partner lists.
- Clear reservation request expectations.

Copy:

> If a partnership does not improve the guest experience and send the right kind of demand to the hospitality partner, it does not belong on the page.

Visual:

- Dark band with restrained typography.
- Use numbered editorial statements rather than icon cards.
- This can reuse the current root page dark standards section idea, but tighten it around conversion.

### 8. Final CTA And Contact

Purpose:

- Capture serious owners after they understand the value.
- Preserve free registration for people ready to move without talking.

Section title:

> Bring your stay or hospitality venue into the network.

Primary CTA:

- `Contact us`
- Leads to in-page contact form.

Secondary CTA:

- `Register free`
- Leads to `/signup`.

Tertiary CTA:

- `View guest demo`
- Leads to `/demo`.

Contact section requirements:

- Must be rewritten for this product.

## Proposed Page Order

1. Hero.
2. Dual-audience convincing section.
3. Five-step guest flow.
4. Unified owner analytics.
5. Custom partnerships.
6. Reservation management.
7. Trust standards.
8. Final CTA and contact.

This order sells the product from emotional clarity to practical proof:

- What it is.
- Why both sides need it.
- How the guest experiences it.
- What owners get after launch.
- Why the network is trustworthy.
- How to start.

## Copy Rules

Use:

- Direct, premium, practical language.
- "Accommodation owners" and "hospitality owners" when clarity matters.
- "Guests already nearby" for hospitality demand.
- "Register free" for accommodations.
- "Curated local benefits" for guest value.
- "Reservation requests" rather than "bookings" when describing unconfirmed reservations.

Avoid:

- "Marketplace" as the main public word unless needed.
- "Enormous amount" in final copy.
- "Hospitalities" as a visitor-facing plural if it sounds awkward. Prefer "hospitality venues", "restaurants, bars, spas, tours, and experiences", or "partner venues".
- "Completed reservation" if the product state is pending. Use "request sent", "pending", or "confirmed" depending on the actual state.

## Acceptance Criteria

The final landing page should pass these checks:

- A first-time visitor can explain the product after the hero and the next section.
- Accommodation owners understand registration is free and guest benefits are the hook.
- Hospitality owners understand they receive nearby guest demand and reservation requests.
- The five-step flow clearly shows QR, stay page, hospitality page, reservation form, and request state.
- Analytics are unified under one feature story without pretending both owners see identical metrics.
- Custom partnerships are explained as curated, specific pairings.
- Reservation status is described accurately as request sent, pending, confirmed, declined, cancelled, or no-show.
- Contact is the primary conversion and free signup remains visible.
- The page feels premium enough for large accommodation and hospitality owners.
- No fake performance claims are used.
- No unrelated contact copy remains.
- Desktop and mobile layouts have no overlapping text or clipped CTAs.
